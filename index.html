About

Hello, I'm
A.M. Cunning

A.M. Cunning is best known for When the Earth Had a Moon (Parts 1 & 2), a space thriller where cutting-edge science collides with global power struggles — crafting stories that feel as urgent as tomorrow's headlines.

Alongside a growing catalog of thought-provoking science fiction, A.M. Cunning is developing a series of children's books designed to spark wonder — stories that plant big ideas in young imaginations and refuse to talk down to the readers who'll inherit the future.

When not writing, Cunning can be found deep in the rabbit hole of world events, emerging technology, and the endlessly fascinating question of where humanity goes from here — passions that also fuel an expanding body of digital content, including vertical shorts and film projects that bring these ideas to life on screen.

The Story

About the Books

Book One · Science Fiction · Space Opera · Thriller

When the Earth Had a Moon — Part 1

"In outer space, there is nowhere to run."

The world's first moon base promised peace. Instead, it became the flashpoint for global annihilation. When nuclear physicist Rex begins searching for his missing fiancée, he uncovers military secrets that put everything — and everyone — at risk.

MoonX Ltd. — a joint venture between American, Chinese, and Russian governments — was built on the dream of peaceful cooperation. But political tensions have a way of following humanity wherever it goes, even 238,000 miles from Earth.

With only his A.I. companion to trust, Rex must navigate a web of military secrets in the most isolated place in the solar system — as the fate of the planet hangs in the balance.

Book Two · Science Fiction · Space Opera · Thriller

When the Earth Had a Moon — Part 2

"In outer space, there is nowhere to run but home."

The moon is gone. The mission is over. Survival has just begun.

After the catastrophic destruction of the lunar base, nuclear physicist Rex finds himself stranded aboard a Russian space station — one that's starting to feel less like sanctuary and more like a trap. With supplies dwindling and Earth growing more distant by the day, Rex and the surviving Moon-X crew must find a way home.

But returning to Earth won't be easy. The shattered remains of the moon now orbit the planet in a deadly halo of debris, blocking every safe path back. Time is running out, and the team must risk everything to escape — without knowing what kind of world still awaits them below.

In this gripping conclusion to the When the Earth Had a Moon saga, survival takes center stage — and the truth of what happened to the moon could be more dangerous than anything in space.
